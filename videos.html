Motion Design and Graphics

NYC Dragon Attack

NYC Dragon Attack is a short motion video demo made in Adobe AfterEffects as part of IMS 228: Motion Designer's Toolkit. This specific demo demonstrates some specific parts of AfterEffects, including layering, effects, lighting, keying, compositions (such as pre-compositions), inclusion of video and sound, and moving images.

BORK

BORK is a short motion video demo made in Adobe AfterEffects as part of IMS 228: Motion Designer's Toolkit. This specific demo dives further into the concepts of layering, lighting, and effects, but also includes new concepts, such as movement in 3D space, scene lights, particles, and more in-depth effects.

Freelance Projects

Growth Mindset Video

Growth Mindset in the Age of Digital Transformation (2019) is a video that was made as part of the Lockheed Martin Leadership Institute's digital transformation social media campaign. This video was created to be more of an informational and serious video, showung viewers what it means to have a growth mindset in today's digital age. For this project, I was in charge of filming and editing. I wanted to convey through video the stark contrast between a positive mindset and a stagnant one; thus, I used a black-and-white filter on the fixed perspective scenes. I also tried to make the two mindsets appear to be "mirror opposites" by filming and editing the scenes to face in different directions.

JEDI 2016 - Teaser Video

From 2014 - 2016, I worked with the non-profit group PARTY (Putnam Adolescent Response Team for Youth) as the Media and Technology Coordinator. PARTY's main goal is to promote healthy life decisions for teens and pre-teens in the Northwest Ohio area through events and campaigns. Our main event was a yearly retreat called JEDI (Juvenile Educational Development Institute). For each JEDI, my job was to create three videos: a teaser video, a rules video, and a closing video.

In general, the JEDI teaser videos (like this one) were probably my favorite to make. When creating them, we had only four stipulations: say what, when, where, and what theme JEDI is that year. Besideds that, we had complete creative liberty to make the teasers entertaining. Of the three teasers I did, this one by far my favorite. To me, it's the funniest of the three, and it also has the most experimental special effects, including the mock Pokémon battle (from Ruby / Sapphire / Emerald), the first (and only) use of a large greenscreen in the JEDI videos, and my first attempt at musically scoring a video (in the beginning).

JEDI 2016 - Rules Video

From 2014 - 2016, I worked with the non-profit group PARTY (Putnam Adolescent Response Team for Youth) as the Media and Technology Coordinator. PARTY's main goal is to promote healthy life decisions for teens and pre-teens in the Northwest Ohio area through events and campaigns. Our main event was a yearly retreat called JEDI (Juvenile Educational Development Institute). For each JEDI, my job was to create three videos: a teaser video, a rules video, and a closing video.

The JEDI rules videos (like this one) were shown to participants at the beginning of JEDI to establish the basic ground rules. When making these videos, we usually either had two choices of what to do: either make a continuous story with rules in between, or have short skits for each rules. I preferred to make one with a continuous story, which is why this one is my favorite of the three I made. Additionally, like the teaser video, we had lots of creative liberties with this video.
