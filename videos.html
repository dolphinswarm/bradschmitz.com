Landward Ho!

Landward Ho! (2017) is a composition for symphony orchestra which was one of my first attempts at writing film score-esque music. When writing this piece, I wanted to capture the exhilirating thrill of adventure, as if the listener is setting sail to embark on a journey to discover new lands and opportunities. The piece is also written like something of a short story: it starts off with the listener leaving port and starting their journey. However, a short way into the jounrey (~1:05), the listener becomes a little homesick, but continues their adventure regardless (~1:30). The journey then quiets down a little (~2:14), until the listener sights new land and heads to begin a new adventury (~2:41); hence, "Landward Ho!".

Alien Landscape

Alien Landscape (2018) is definitely a lot different than the other pieces on this page, in that it's not what would be considered a typical "song". This piece was written in one of my classes (MUS 221H: Music Technologies) as one of our projects, and is meant to give the feeling of a strage, foreign soundscape the listener is not used to. I chose to make my piece sound like an "alien jungle", with lots of digital audio effects and sounds (such as chirping birds, running water, ringing bells, etc.) to make the piece seem vaguely familiar, yet still "alien". Synthetic instruments are also used to help create tension throughout the piece.

Devoid

Devoid (2015) is a pretty simple piece for piano written in G minor (my favorite key!). Technically, this piece was written for a scrapped game called Galaxy Wars as somber background music, but I ended up liking it so much that I changed it to a standalone concert piece. When writing the piece, I tried to give the title two separate meanings. First, I wanted it to feel as the title implies: devoid and empty. Particularly in the beginning and end, I used lots of long pauses between note groups to make the song cold and contemplative. However, this is sort of inverted in the middle for the second meaning of the piece: removing the void (de-voiding, if you will). The climax of the piece has lots of fast notes and loud chords, which, in conjunction with a more heroic sound, help fill the emptiness of the void.

Video Game Music

Galaxy Wars - Main Theme

Galaxy Wars - Main Theme (2017) is, as the title implies, the main theme for a scrapped video game I was working on called Galaxy Wars. When working on video games, I have a nasty habit of wanting to write the music before completing the framework of the game. This piece is a prime example of that: it was meant to play on the title screen and a few other areas of the game, but it was written before any place it could be used was created. Maybe one day I'll keep working on the game and be able to use this theme?
