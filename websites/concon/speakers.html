Featured Speakers

Dr. Peter Coogan

Director of the Institute for Comics Studies and co-founder and co-chair of the Comics Arts Conference

Peter Coogan is the founder of the Institute for Comics Studies, which was created to "promote the study, understanding, recognition, and cultural legitimacy of comics." Coogan is also the co-founder (along with Randy Duncan), co-chair, and co-organizer of the Comics Arts Conference, which is held every year at the San Diego Comic-Con International and at WonderCon to "bring together comics scholars, practitioners, critics, and historians who want to be involved in the dynamic process of evolving an aesthetic and a criticism of the comics art form." Additionally, Coogan has authored two books, Superhero: The Secret Origin of a Genre (2006) and What is a Superhero? (2003)

Dr. Ann Kurth

Dean and the Linda Koch Lorimer Professor of Nursing at Yale University, Chair of the Consortium of Universities for Global Health

Ann Kurth, PhD, CNM, MPH, FAAN is the Dean and Linda Koch Lorimer Professor at the Yale University School of Nursing. Kurth's research, which focuses on HIV/reproductive health and global health systems, has been the subject of over 175 peer-reviewed articles, book chapters, and scholarly monographs, and Kurth has presented at hundreds of conferences. In addition to her position on the Consortium of Universities for Global Health, Kurth is also an elected Fellow of the Institute of Medicine (National Academy of Medicine) and a member of the 2014-2018 US Preventive Services Task Force.



Gary Shapiro

President and CEO of the Consumer Technology Association

Gary Shapiro is the current president and CEO of the Consumer Technology Association, which hosts the International Consumer Electronic Show annually in Las Vegas. Shapiro authored of the New York Times best-selling books Ninja Innovation: The Ten Killer Strategies of the World’s Most Successful Businesses (2013) and The Comeback: How Innovation Will Restore the American Dream (2011). Shapiro has been called “one of the most influential lobbyists” in Washington, D.C., for his work in promoting business and technology for Congress.
