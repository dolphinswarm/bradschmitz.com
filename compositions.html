Orchestral Music

Forge

If the embed doesn't display, you can view the score here!

Forge (2020) is a composition for symphony orchestra. This song is meant to convey the dark and metallic nature of an evil metal forge, which is accomplished through the heavy use of percussion (including an anvil) and lots of deep instruments and voices (including a male choir). I wanted this song to be very ominous and driving, so I started off with a slower section, which quickly builds into a repeated rhyrhtmic motif. However, during the bridge sections, I also wanted to convey a slight amount of heroism and melancholy to contrast the evil nature of the rest of the piece.

Landward Ho!

If the embed doesn't display, you can view the score here!

Landward Ho! (2017) is a composition for symphony orchestra which was one of my first attempts at writing film score-esque music. When writing this piece, I wanted to capture the exhilirating thrill of adventure, as if the listener is setting sail to embark on a journey to discover new lands and opportunities. The piece is also written like something of a short story: it starts off with the listener leaving port and starting their journey. However, a short way into the jounrey (~1:05), the listener becomes a little homesick, but continues their adventure regardless (~1:30). The journey then quiets down a little (~2:14), until the listener sights new land and heads to begin a new adventury (~2:41); hence, "Landward Ho!".

Devoid

If the embed doesn't display, you can view the score here!

Devoid (2015) is a duet (or optionally solo) piece for piano written in G minor (my favorite key!). When writing the piece, I tried to give the title two separate meanings. First, I wanted it to feel as the title implies: devoid and empty. Particularly in the beginning and end, I used lots of long pauses between note groups to make the song cold and contemplative. However, this is sort of inverted in the middle for the second meaning of the piece: removing the void (de-voiding, if you will). The climax of the piece has lots of fast notes and loud chords, which, in conjunction with a more heroic sound, help fill the emptiness of the void.

Electronic Music

From The Ground Up

From The Ground Up (2019) was a piece written as the final project for MUS / IMS 306: Advanced Electronic Music. One of the the unique things about this piece is that is is nearly all the sounds utilized in it were synthesized by me (through the help of a Doepfer Analog Modular System), including the drums, instruments, and effects; hence, "From the Ground Up". However, the title also refers to the ascension throughout the piece, from quick, spy-esque song to heavenly chorus.

Alien Landscape

Alien Landscape (2018) was an electroacoustic piece written for MUS 221H: Music Technologies. As is the case with most electroacoustic pieces, it is meant to give the feeling of a strage, foreign soundscape the listener is not used to. I chose to make my piece sound like an "alien jungle", with lots of digital audio effects and sounds (such as chirping birds, running water, ringing bells, etc.) to make the piece seem vaguely familiar, yet still "alien". Synthetic instruments are also used to help create tension throughout the piece.

Video Game Music

Galaxy Wars

Galaxy Wars - Main Theme (2017) was the main theme for a scrapped video game I was working on. While the final video game never saw the light of day, the main theme is here to stay. Maybe one day I'll resume work on the game and be able to use this theme?

Empty Town

Empty Town (2015) is a song that would play in an abandoned town or building level in a video game. This song is pretty similar to Devoid above, as it is also in G minor, uses similar motifs, and is meant to convey emptiness. How this song differs is in its compositional style. When writing the piece, I wanted to experiment with odd, changing time signatures (switching between 3/4, 4/4, and 5/4, mainly) and a mix of traditional / synthetic instruments.

Roots (Botanical Center)

Roots (Botanical Center) (2014) is a song that would play in a jungle level. This song is very percussion-heavy and relies a lot on timpani, a marimba motif, and congra drums to keep the beat driving. To keep the song simple, I only a couple of other instruments in the song - French Horn, bass guitar, strings, whistle, tenor sax, xylophone, and suspended cymbal.

Final Castle

Final Castle (2013) is the theme for a final level of a game. This song is meant to sound very regal and epic, with the heavy use of powerful brass, soaring strings, and echoing choir. However, the song is also meant to convey feelings of melancholy as well (as evidenced by the slower section of the song).

Pixel Jet - Main Theme

Pixel Jet - Main Theme (2013) is the main theme for a Scratch game I made for the National STEM Video Game Challenge called Pixel Jetd. This song is actually a chiptune remix of a song (called Colosseum) originally intended for another game, and was used as a placeholder song for the title screen while I worked on an actual title theme. However, I ended up liking this song more than the WIP title theme, and so this song became the new title theme. The chiptune remix was created with a chiptune-writing program called FamiTracker.

Pixel Jet - Battle Theme

Pixel Jet - Battle Theme (2013) is the theme for the Arcade Mode mode (the standard mode) in Pixel Jet. Interestingly enough, this song was not written in FamiTracker like Pixel Jet - Main Theme, and was instead created by composing a MIDI file and using a program called GXSCC to give each instrument a chiptune soundfont. Admittedly, the chiptune sound of GXCSS is less authentic, but it is much easier to use than FamiTracker when composing new songs.
